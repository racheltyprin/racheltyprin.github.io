HUMAN COMPUTER INTERACTION RESEARCH

"Needed This": Hopecore and the Case for Positive Information Online
Published in the Journal of Documentation, 2026
Co-authored with Tim Gorichanaz, Drexel University

———

Social media is implicated in the growing mental health crisis. Platforms are built to maximize engagement, and the content that drives engagement tends to exploit negative emotions like outrage and anxiety. Occasionally, social media produces counter-currents worth paying attention to, like Hopecore. Hopecore is a social media trend centered on spreading optimism, emotional reassurance, and collective care through short-form content, with posts often containing uplifting text overlays, nature scenes, or moments of everyday kindness. By studying how hopeful content in the hopecore trend emerges and resonates within existing algorithmic ecosystems, this research explores whether social media might be redesigned to better support emotional well-being rather than undermine it.

We analyzed 244 hopecore posts and 450 user comments on Instagram, coding each by visual content, message category, and type of hope-conducive information. Drawing on psychological and philosophical frameworks, we evaluated hopecore against three components of hope: believing a positive outcome is possible, feeling motivated to pursue it, and having a concrete pathway for getting there. 72% of comments were positive in valence, and many went beyond simple reactions: users shared personal reflections, articulated goals, and expressed renewed motivation. This pointed clearly to the first two components of hope, but rarely the third, suggesting an opportunity for platforms to better support users in translating hopeful thinking into action.

Platforms could act on these findings in three ways: surfacing positive content algorithmically to users showing signs of distress, encouraging reflection and active engagement with comment prompts, and building community features that connect users around shared hopeful themes. By understanding why hopecore resonates with users, we can begin to imagine social media platforms that are intentionally designed to cultivate hope, reflection, and positive connection.
