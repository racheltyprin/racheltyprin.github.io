AI PROTOTYPING CART

A structured toolkit helping Stanford design students navigate AI tools
throughout every phase of the product design process.

———

The Context

The students in this design class are domain experts. PhDs in engineering, education, medicine learning how to turn deep technical knowledge into products that serve everyday people. The gap was that building prototypes typically requires fluency in tools like Figma, web development, or other software these students hadn't needed before. They were working with pen, paper, and scissors while a growing landscape of AI tools could let them prototype at much higher fidelity. They just needed to know which tools existed, which ones fit their phase of work, and how to actually use them.

The Approach

The people in this class had knowledge that could really help people, they just needed the right tools to build with. Starting from a brief given by the course instructor, I adapted the design methodology the students were following into a three-phase design process: research, prototyping, and MVP development. To do this, I needed to familiarize myself with each tool and understand not just what it did, but which phase it served and how a non-technical user could actually pick it up. The categories spanned research and synthesis, UI prototyping, image generation, video and audio, and MVP creation. I built a first version, tested it with 12 students, and iterated on their feedback, adding search functionality and specific tools they requested.

The Results

Users rated navigation 4.4/5 and content helpfulness 4.2/5. Feedback pointed toward wanting more depth: example projects, concrete use cases, and tighter workflow integration. This feedback provides a clear roadmap for version 2 if the project continues.
